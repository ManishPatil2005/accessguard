# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We require all contributors to agree with and adhere to the following code of conduct.

---

## Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment include:

- **Be Respectful**: Treat all community members with courtesy and respect.
- **Be Inclusive**: Welcoming newcomers and providing help to get them started.
- **Be Patient**: Remember that people have different communication styles and levels of understanding.
- **Be Professional**: Focus discussions on the code and ideas, not on personal attacks.
- **Be Constructive**: Provide thoughtful, constructive feedback.
- **Give Credit**: Acknowledge and appreciate contributions from others.

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Harassment**: Comments, jokes, or advances of a sexual nature.
- **Discrimination**: Based on age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender, nationality, religion, sexual identity, or sexual orientation.
- **Bullying**: Deliberate intimidation or threats.
- **Trolling**: Intentional disruption or inflammatory remarks.
- **Doxxing**: Publishing private information without consent.
- **Spamming**: Repetitive, unsolicited messages.
- **Unwelcome Sexual Content**: Any sexualized comments, images, or behavior.

---

## Enforcement

Community members who violate this code of conduct may be:
- Asked to apologize
- Temporarily suspended
- Permanently banned from the project

Violations can be reported to the project maintainers.

---

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:
- Clarifying standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate corrective action for unacceptable behavior
- Removing posts or comments that violate this code of conduct
- Protecting the confidentiality of reporters

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- GitHub issues, pull requests, and discussions
- Comments and code reviews
- Community Discord/Chat (if applicable)
- Project meetings and events
- Any official AccessGuard communication channels
